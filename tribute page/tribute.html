Who is Steve Jobs?

Steve Jobs is an American business magnate, industrial designer, investor, and media owner. He is the chairman, chief executive (CEO), and co-founder of Apple Inc., chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar, member of the board of directors of The Walt Disney Company following the acquisition of Pixar, and founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT. Jobs is widely recognized as the pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.

Steve Jobs Timeline

  • 1976

    Co-founder of Apple Computer with Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. That same year, the company sold the Apple I in a kit form that retailed for $666.66.

  • 5 June 1977

    Released the Apple II, the first commercially available personal computer in a plastic case with color graphics - and Apple's first successful personal computer.

  • 24 January 1984

    Two days after Ridley Scott's $1.5 million "1984" Super Bowl commercial, introduced the Macintosh to much fanfare during an Apple shareholder meeting. "For the first time, I wanted the Macintosh to speak for itself." The computer voice then said, "Never trust a computer you can't pick up." The Macintosh became the first commercially successful small computer with a graphical user interface.

  • 12 September 1985

    CEO John Sculley engineered Jobs' sacking from Apple. Jobs stepped down as chairman of Apple, telling a board meeting, "I've been thinking a lot, and it's time for me to move on with my life. Obviously I had to do something. I was 30 years old." Soon after, Jobs started NeXT Computer ( which later became NeXT Software), was funded by selling its $70 million Apple stock. An "interpersonal" NeXT workstation, using a built-in Ethernet port, was used by Tim Berners-Lee at CERN to become the first server of the World Wide Web.

  • 3 February 1986

    For $10 million, buy the Graphics Group division of Lucasfilm that became Pixar Animation Studios.

  • 1976

    Become president and CEO of Pixar. Later that year, Jobs presented the Pixar pubic one week after the release of "Toy Story," with Tom Hanks voicing Woody and Tim Allen as Buzz Lightyear. The film earned $192 million at the box office. Its success helped make it attractive enough for celebrities to voice their voices to animated characters.

  • 10 December 1996

    Returned to Apple, as an advisor, after buying NeXT for $429 million.

  • 10 November 1997

    Introducing the Apple Store, which allows consumers to order custom Apple products directly from the company online.

  • 23 October 2001

    Introducing iPod:

    "With iPod, Apple has invented a whole new category of digital music player that lets you put your entire music collection in your pocket and listen to it wherever you go...With iPod, listening to music will never be the same again."

    Apple shipped more than 300 million iPods in October 2011.

  • 2 February 2006

    iTunes sells a billionth song:

    "I hope that every customer, artist and music company executive takes a moment today to reflect on what we've achieved together during the past three years."

    Apple mengirimkan lebih dari 300 juta iPod pada Oktober 2011.

  • 9 January 2007

    Dropped "Computer" from the Apple name. Introducing iPhone:

    "iPhone is a revolutionary and magical product that is literally five years ahead of any other mobile phone...We are all born with the ultimate pointing device--our fingers--and iPhone uses them to create the most revolutionary user interface since the mouse."

    Apple menguasai 19,1 persen pasar ponsel pintar per Agustus 2011, setelah mengapalkan 108 juta iPhone ke seluruh dunia pada Maret 2011.

  • 15 January 2008

    Introducing the App Store as an update to iTunes. Also introduced the MacBook Air. The App Store facilitated 18 billion app downloads as of October 4, 2011.

  • 27 January 2010

    Introducing the iPad. Apple sold 500,000 iPads during their first week on the market and accounted for 84 percent of the tablet market by the end of the year, with an estimated 12.9 million shipped on December 10, 2010, and 25 million shipped worldwide in June 2011. That same month, the company revealed that they had sold 200 million iOS devices.

  • 6 June 2011

    Giving the keynote address at Apple's Worldwide Developers Conference, this was Jobs' last public appearance at an Apple event.

  • 9 August 2011

    Apple shares overtook Exxon Mobil to become the most valuable US company, in terms of market capitalization, at $337.17 billion.

  • 24 August 2011

    Resigned from the position of CEO of Apple; become chairman.

"For the past 33 years, I have looked in the mirror every morning and asked myself: 'If today were the last day of my life, would I want to do what I am about to do today?' And whenever the answer has been 'No' for too many days in a row, I know I need to change something."
